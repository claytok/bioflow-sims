Mathematical Models

Fick's First Law:
J = -D × ∇C
Flux = -Diffusion coefficient × Concentration gradient
Fick's Second Law:
∂C/∂t = D × ∇²C
Rate of concentration change = D × Laplacian of concentration
Temperature Effect:
D = D₀ × exp(Eₐ/R × (1/T₀ - 1/T))
Diffusion increases with temperature
Stokes-Einstein Relation:
D = kT/(6πηr)
D inversely proportional to molecular radius

What you're seeing:

  • Concentration gradient: Molecules spread from high concentration (left) to low concentration (right)
  • Scenario-specific colors: Each simulation uses different colors to represent different molecules
  • Speed differences: O₂ (red) spreads very fast, drugs (purple) spread very slowly - try switching scenarios!
  • Diffusion over time: Molecules spread from high to low concentration
  • Temperature effect: Higher temperature increases diffusion rate
  • Size effect: Larger molecules diffuse more slowly

Key concept: Diffusion is the net movement of molecules from high to low concentration!
